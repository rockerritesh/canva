What is a Battery?

A battery is an electrochemical device that converts chemical energy into electrical energy through redox reactions.

  • Anode: Negative terminal where oxidation occurs
  • Cathode: Positive terminal where reduction occurs
  • Electrolyte: Medium for ion transport
  • External Circuit: Path for electron flow

Types of Batteries

Primary Batteries

Single-use, non-rechargeable

Examples: Alkaline, Zinc-Carbon

Secondary Batteries

Rechargeable, reversible reactions

Examples: Li-ion, NiMH, Lead-acid

Lithium-Ion Batteries

  • High energy density: 150-250 Wh/kg
  • Long cycle life: 500-2000 cycles
  • Low self-discharge: ~5% per month
  • Voltage: 3.6-3.7V nominal

Key Battery Parameters

  • Capacity: Total charge a battery can store
  • Voltage: Electrical potential difference

  • C-Rate: 1C = full discharge in 1 hour
  • Cycle Life: Number of charge-discharge cycles
